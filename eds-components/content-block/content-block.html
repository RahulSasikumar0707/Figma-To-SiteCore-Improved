Who should not take DESCOVY for PrEP?

If you think you might have been exposed to HIV before starting or while taking DESCOVY for PrEP, let a healthcare provider know right away. DESCOVY by itself is not a complete treatment for HIV—in fact, taking DESCOVY alone to treat HIV can make HIV harder to treat now and in the future. Do not take DESCOVY for PrEP if you weigh less than 77 pounds.

Creating a healthier world for all people

HIV (human immunodeficiency virus) is a virus that attacks the human immune system. Unlike some other viruses, such as the common cold, HIV cannot be cleared from the body. There is no cure for HIV, but there are treatments available. Talk to your healthcare provider and learn more about a treatment option.

How is HIV transmitted?

HIV is transmitted through contact with certain body fluids, such as semen, vaginal or anal fluids, breast milk, and blood. Contact with these body fluids can occur during unprotected sex or when sharing needles or other items with body fluids on them. Mothers can pass the HIV virus to their babies during pregnancy, birth, and breastfeeding. HIV CANNOT be transmitted through contact with sweat, tears, saliva, bath or pool water, or by insect bite, coughing, sneezing, drinking from a water fountain, sharing dishes or drinking glasses, hugging, kissing, shaking hands, or eating foods prepared by someone living with HIV

How can HIV affect your body?

HIV attacks and destroys CD4 T-cells — important immune system cells that help the body fight infections. The more CD4 T-cells that are destroyed, the weaker the immune system can become. With fewer CD4 T-cells due to HIV, it can be harder for the body to fight illnesses and infections.

Understanding DESCOVY for PrEP®

DESCOVY for PrEP (pre-exposure prophylaxis) is a once-daily prescription medicine for adults and adolescents at risk of HIV. It helps lower the chances of getting HIV through sex.
